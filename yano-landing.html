Why we exist

Loneliness doesn't announce itself.

It shows up as a quiet evening that goes on too long. A day you got through but didn't really talk about. Most people carry it alone — not because there's no one who'd listen, but because saying it out loud to someone who knows you feels harder than saying nothing at all.

Bak Bak isn't therapy and it isn't dating. It's a scheduled phone call with a real person whose only job, for those twenty minutes, is to listen. You choose the time. You choose how much to say.

Let's be clear

Not therapy. Not a helpline. Just a friend on the phone.

Most apps built for men's emotional wellbeing lead with doctors, counselors, and clinical language. That's the right thing for some problems — but not every bad day needs a professional. Sometimes you just want to rant about your boss, complain about your in-laws, or talk through whether you should take the promotion — without booking a "session" or explaining a diagnosis.

Bak Bak is trained women who are there to talk like a friend would — not to treat you, assess you, or take notes on you. No forms, no "how does that make you feel." Just a conversation, the way you'd have it with someone who already knows you, except you don't have to explain the backstory.
